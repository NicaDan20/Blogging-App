What is IT compliance?
The line between IT security and IT compliance is easily blurred. Compliance is often misunderstood as being synonimous with data protection but that is not the case. While the branch of IT Security deals with the implementation of strategies and technologies that protect your data from malicious attacks, IT Compliance is defined as a set of rules, principles and standards a company must meet in order to operate legally. The requirements relate to the measures used for implementing IT Security strategies in your company. Most compliance requirements stem from legal bases or other official regulations and are absolutely mandatory in order to have your business' activity approved. If companies violate these regulations, it can be punished with heavy fines and even imprisonment for the responsible parties.

Who needs compliance?

Basically, every company and organization using an IT System (which basically means all) must adhere to IT Compliance requirements. The relevant government authorities define the IT Compliance regulations. Of course, the regulations that a company must comply with differ depending on the field of work, company size, number of customers and overall societal role of each company. For example, the strictest IT Compliance will apply to companies including but not limited to the energy, healthcare, food, transport, government, finance and insurance sectors.

Compliance for such companies is extremely extensive and will often require help from 3rd party providers to meet all the requirements. In general, companies are subject to random compliance checks by supervisory authorities.

  • HIPAA - Healthcare Insurance Portability and Accountability Act is a federal law enacted in 1996, aimed at protecting the confdientiality of a patient's healthcare information and assist the healthcare industry control administrative costs.   • FINRA - Financial Industry Regulatory Authority is a private American Corporation that acts as a self regulatory organization that regulates member brokerage firms and exchange markets. FINRA is the largest independent regulator for security firms in the United States. FINRA regulates trading in equities, corporate bonds, securities futures and options and has been authorized by the US Congress to protect the interests of investors.
  • PCI-DSS - Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard is administered by the PCI SSC (Payment Card Industry Security Standards Council) to decrease payment card fraud across the Internet and increase payment card data security. PCI-DSS compliance is important as it helps ensure a secure environment for organizations that process, store, or transmit credit card information. If implemented correctly, the PCI DSS can help organizations secure cardholder data. It provides a baseline set of security requirements that lets organizations know what action they should take.
